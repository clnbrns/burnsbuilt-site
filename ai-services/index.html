What we actually build

Six categories of production AI work. Each is something we've shipped or could ship inside a week.

SHIPPED AI Lead Qualifier

Form submission → Claude scores 1–100 + drafts personalized reply + texts you the analysis. We run this on burnsbuilt.co itself. Going from "I'll respond within a business day" to "I responded in 8 minutes with a tailored quote" is the difference between losing leads and closing them.

Setup: $500–$1,500 · Run cost: ~$1.30/mo per 100 leads · 1–2 days to ship

SHIPPED AI bid engines / quote calculators

Customer emails a 12-page PDF (architectural plan, scope of work, RFP). Claude with vision extracts dimensions, materials, quantities into structured data. Your bid calculator runs the math against historical cost data. Output: an accurate bid in 15 seconds. Bearcat Estimator is the canonical example.

Setup: $3,000–$8,000 depending on document complexity · Run cost: ~$0.05–$0.10 per document

SHIPPED Dual-LLM marketing platforms

Every email goes to Claude and Gemini in parallel, gets scored against a heuristic rubric, and the higher-scoring draft is presented as primary with the alternate one click away. MSM Marketing Engine shipped 121 campaigns and 100,306 emails this way, with a 56.6% average open rate.

Setup: $5,000–$10,000+ · Run cost: ~$20–$50/mo at moderate volume

PROVEN Missed-call AI summarizer

Customer leaves a voicemail. AI listens, transcribes, and texts you a plain-English summary so you don't have to listen. Combined with missed-call text-back ("Hey, sorry we missed you"), most service businesses recover 30%+ of missed calls.

Setup: $500–$1,500 · Run cost: ~$5/mo at moderate call volume

PROVEN Internal data analytics in plain English

Your customer database, invoicing, and campaign data wired into an LLM. Ask: "What's my best month-over-month trend? Which service has the highest margin? Which customer hasn't booked in 6 months?" Get actual answers from your real data, in 30 seconds, from your phone.

Setup: $2,000–$8,000 depending on data complexity · Run cost: ~$20–$60/mo

PROVEN Customer support chatbot (with guardrails)

Chat widget on your site that answers FAQs using your actual content. Bounded — only answers from your knowledge base, escalates to a form fill or call when it can't. Handles 80%+ of the common questions, frees you up for the ones that need a human.

Setup: $500–$2,000 · Run cost: ~$10–$30/mo

Frequently asked

What kinds of AI tools do you actually build?

Production-grade AI integrations for specific business problems: lead qualification (we run our own — see the AI Lead Qualifier), automated customer response drafting, PDF/document parsing for quotes and invoices, custom CRMs with natural-language search, internal analytics chatbots, and dual-LLM ensemble marketing platforms. We don't build generic chatbots or "AI sales agents" — those don't work in production.

Which AI models do you use?

Anthropic Claude (Sonnet 4.5/4.6/4.7 for nuanced work, Haiku for cheap classification), Google Gemini (Pro for multimodal, Flash for high-volume), and OpenAI when fit-for-purpose. For most small-business use cases Claude Sonnet is the right default — about $0.005 per typical API call. Most of our internal tools (MSM Marketing Engine) use a dual-LLM ensemble, calling two models in parallel and presenting the higher-scoring output.

How much does an AI feature cost to build and run?

Setup: $500 for a single-feature integration (e.g. AI lead qualifier), $1,500–$5,000 for multi-feature workflows, $5,000+ for full custom AI platforms like MSM Marketing Engine. Variable cost: typically $5–$50/month in API fees for most small business volumes. A 100-lead/month operation running an AI lead qualifier costs about $1.30 in Claude API fees.

Do I need to be technical to use what you build?

No. Every AI tool we build runs in the background of your existing workflow. Lead qualifier fires when your form gets a submission. PDF parser runs when a customer emails you a quote request. You don't "use" AI — the AI is just there, doing the work invisibly. The only thing you "use" is the output: a drafted email, a scored lead, a parsed bid.

How are you different from the "AI agency" down the street?

Three differences. First, we build for small businesses, not enterprise — our pricing reflects that ($500–$10K, not $50K+). Second, we ship working AI in production, not slide decks (see MSM Marketing Engine: 100,306 emails sent, 56.6% open rate). Third, we don't sell autonomous "AI sales agents" or magic chatbots — we build focused integrations that solve specific bottlenecks with humans still in the loop.
