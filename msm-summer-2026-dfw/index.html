House Rules

How we keep it fun.

This is about the kids and the friendships. Three roles, one standard — respect the game, the umpires, and each other.

For Parents & Spectators

  • Cheer for your team. Never against the other team or the umpires.
  • Trust your coach. Coaching from the stands creates confused players.
  • Disputes go through the head coach — never directly to umpires or opposing coaches.
  • Keep language and gestures appropriate. Players hear everything.
  • Respect the venue: clean up your area, use trash cans, no alcohol on grounds.
  • Be a model of good sportsmanship. Your kid is watching.

For Coaches

  • You set the tone. Calm bench, calm dugout, calm parents.
  • All umpire conversations: through the head coach, between innings, in a respectful tone.
  • Lineup cards delivered to the table 10 minutes before first pitch.
  • Pitch counts logged after every appearance per MSM/age-group rules.
  • No coach is permitted on the field of play during action.
  • Address parent issues privately, after the game. Not in the dugout.
  • Tournament director's decisions are final.

For Players

  • Hustle on and off the field. Every inning, every game.
  • Respect umpires — even on calls you don't agree with. Coach handles disputes.
  • No throwing equipment. No profanity. No taunting. One warning, then ejection.
  • Help your teammates. Pick up bats, hand out helmets, call out plays.
  • Shake hands after every game. Win or lose.
  • Have fun. This is the part you'll remember.

Quick Answers

What other parents ask.

How were teams formed?

We look at a few factors. First is sign-up time — we like folks who sign up early. Then we look at pitchers and catchers; we had to move quite a few kids around because some teams just simply didn't have enough catchers and/or pitchers. Then we also look at competitive balance. Lastly, we try to keep kids at certain schools together so one kid isn't a floater. It's not a perfect science, and we do our best.

How was the schedule made?

We tried to schedule it competitively so no team went 0–2 if we could avoid that. But this is still youth baseball and things happen. Ultimately we want this to be a competitively fair tournament where everyone feels like they had a shot to go deep in bracket play.

What time should we arrive?

Please be early. If we can start games early, we will.

Can I bring outside food and coolers?

Yes. Coolers and outside food/drinks are typically allowed at Parks at Texas Star. Concessions are also available on-site.

What happens if there's a weather delay?

The alert ribbon at the top of this page updates with weather holds and field changes. You can also opt in for SMS alerts below.

Are the photos really free?

Yes. The photos in our gallery are free for parents to download at full resolution. Geis Photography offers separate paid posed sessions if you want professional team or individual portraits.

How do I find my team's field?

Field assignments are in the live Exposure Events schedule embedded above. Cross-check with the on-site board when you arrive — assignments occasionally shift due to weather.

Where do I park?

On-site parking at Parks at Texas Star. Overflow parking is signposted at peak times. Use the Apple Maps / Google Maps / Waze links above for live traffic.

What's the refund policy?

No refunds are available.

Who do I contact during the tournament?

Tap the red Help button (bottom right of this page) to text the tournament director directly. SMS only — fastest way to get a response on game day.
