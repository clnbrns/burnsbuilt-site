The Mission

Why MSM exists, and what #happybaseball actually means.

What we're about
  • A positive, competitive, kid-centered environment.
  • Games that are fun — music, laughter, great energy.
  • Respect for umpires, opponents, and the game itself.
  • A preview of what's coming in high school — without the travel-ball grind.
  • Manage more than you coach. Let the kids play.
What MSM is not
  • Not travel ball — by design.
  • No scouts. No rankings. No trophy-drama.
🏫

Team Formation

How Pure Teams and Combo Teams get built.

Pure Teams

The first 9–12 players registered from a school form that school's Pure Team. If a school gets more than 12, we either form a second team (if numbers allow) or shift the overflow to a Combo Team.

Combo Teams

Combos are built by school district first, then geographical proximity, then everything else if needed. Don't sleep on them — some combo teams end up pretty salty, especially in certain boys' divisions.

How priority works

The timestamp of registration is the priority factor. Early sign-ups get first dibs on Pure Team spots. (We're still a business, after all.)

Catcher exceptions

Some exceptions were made to make sure every team had a catcher. If a school's Pure Team would have gone catcher-less, we moved a few players around to keep games playable. No team plays MSM without one behind the plate.

Can rosters change?

No. Rosters lock to preserve the middle-school experience and competitive balance.

Game Operations

How we run the day. Start early, play everyone, keep it moving.

Start early & warm up smart

Packed weekend, lots of games — every minute counts. Warm up while the previous game is going.

Warm-up pitches
  • First inning: up to 8 warm-up pitches + infield.
  • After that: 3 warm-up pitches, no infield.
  • Keep it moving — more innings = more fun.
Playing time

Everyone bats. Play everyone. Make playing time equitable — that's the whole point. Smaller combo teams often mean more reps for everyone.

Music & atmosphere

Music is encouraged. If the field is cool with it, ask a parent to DJ the entire game — including pitching and at-bats. Keep it family-friendly. Think Banana Ball.

Pool play kickoff

Start pool games with a Best-of-3 Rock-Paper-Scissors between the two captains (or the whole teams watching). Make it a moment.

Baseballs

MSM DFW West supplies a box of baseballs for each field each day — should be plenty. Pro move: assign a little brother to chase down foul balls. Keeps the game moving and gives the little guy a job.

Rules Highlights

The coach version. Full rule reference in GameChanger.

Pitch limits (weekend total)
  • 6th grade: 75 pitches
  • 7th grade: 75 pitches
  • 8th grade: 85 pitches

GameChanger pitch tracking is authoritative. Follow posted pitch-count and rest guidelines.

Bats

We follow NCS bat rules. Whatever bats are approved for NCS games are approved for MSM.

Lineup & playing time

Encourage equal opportunity. Everyone bats. Smaller combo teams often equal more reps.

Coaches in the dugout

Three max: 1st base, 3rd base, bench coach.

Sportsmanship & ejections

Zero tolerance for abuse. This is for kids — keep it clean.

Uniforms & Pickup

Where to grab shirts and what your players can wear.

Pickup options
  • TBD North Tarrant County
  • Aledo (home pickup)

A team rep or parent volunteer can handle both pickup and coaches-meeting attendance if needed.

When to hand out shirts

You can distribute player shirts before your first game.

Pants, belts, socks

Doesn't matter — whatever flows with your school colors.

Hats

Players can wear their travel team, rec, high school, or favorite college/pro hat — whatever best represents their school or team pride.

Jersey numbers

All numbers are random. Less thinking, less organizing.

Combo team color clash?

Each player wears their own school's uniform, so combo teams will naturally have a mix of colors. That's part of the fun of it.

Umpires & On-Field Flow

Pro crew, neutral and experienced. Help them help you.

Who's calling games

We're using OTP Umps — the same professional crew that calls NCS games. Experienced, neutral, and there to make the games flow smoothly. If you've coached NCS, you already know what to expect. Treat them with respect.

Speed up the pre-game
  • Have your lineup ready.
  • Ground rules in 60 seconds.
Rules disputes

If there's a rules question, the Head Coach calmly requests time and speaks directly to the Plate Umpire. Not from the dugout, not from the third-base box.

Extras

Walk-up songs, pre-tournament practices, team names, photos.

Walk-up songs & game music

Absolutely — please do! We want the atmosphere fun and high-energy. Keep music playing throughout the game, even during pitching and at-bats. Just keep it family-friendly.

Pre-tournament practice

Yes. Coaches are responsible for securing a field. Most high school coaches are happy to let middle-school teams use their fields, or you can usually rent one through the district for about $50–$100.

Team names

Get creative — but the schedule will include the formal name.

Photos & gallery

Action shots are free in the MSM gallery after each game. Team and individual portrait packages sold direct through geisphoto.com.

Facilities & Safety

Concessions, vendors, and what to do when something goes wrong.

Concessions & vendors
  • Concessions are operated by the park or their approved partners.
  • Outside vendors (shirts, photos, etc.) must be pre-approved by the facility and MSM.
  • Keep dugouts and stands tidy — please police your trash between games.
Injuries & unsafe conditions

Report immediately to the Site Director.

Lightning & weather

In lightning delays or heavy rain, follow staff instructions. We resume only when cleared. Updates posted in GameChanger and texted to head coaches.
